Operating artifact · page 1

Define the work before selecting the AI.

Use this review to turn an idea into a decision-ready work package. Blank or weak fields are not paperwork defects; they are delivery, governance, adoption, or value risks.

1 · Workflow point

What exact artifact, handoff, cadence, exception, or decision changes? What happens immediately before and after?

2 · Business consequence

What rework, delay, quality risk, cost, search burden, or decision burden matters? What baseline exists?

3 · Human authority

What may AI propose or execute? Who approves, overrides, escalates, stops, and remains accountable?

4 · Data and evidence

Which sources are authoritative, permissible, current, traceable, and retained? What conflicts or gaps are expected?

5 · Owner and adoption

Who owns the workflow outcome, support, training, exceptions, correction loop, and post-pilot operation?

6 · Delivery pattern

Guidance, copilot, agent, low-code app, automation, lightweight model, integration, reuse, or no build?

7 · Value receipt

Which evidence supports advance, revise, reuse, hold, stop, or transfer? When will it be reviewed?

8 · Reuse boundary

What is stable across projects? What must remain configurable because of project, customer, location, or contract?
