Bechtel’s AI Enablement Lead is not being hired to create more AI activity. The role exists to make distributed project demand governable, reusable, and measurable—without slowing the engineering and project judgment that protects safety, quality, schedule, and customer commitments.

That mandate matches the work I have repeatedly chosen. At Vape-Jet, I lead AI-first operating transformation across production, purchasing, quality, support, customer success, engineering issue flow, ERP/Odoo, Jira, HubSpot, Slack, telemetry, and machine-fleet feedback. Through DudeWorth, I develop AI opportunity and readiness frameworks that connect business value, feasibility, data, governance, human authority, adoption burden, reuse, and time-to-value. At Compunetics, I led engineering, manufacturing, quality, customer delivery, and complex programs in advanced electronics and high-reliability environments. At Amazon, I built the operating routines, visibility, standard work, and escalation needed for scaled 24/7 delivery.

I would bring Bechtel an operator’s approach to AI enablement: begin with a decision-critical workflow; identify the owner, consequence, evidence, and authority boundary; deliver the smallest controlled intervention; measure what changes in real work; and convert successful elements into reusable standards, training, and portfolio learning. The goal is not a catalog of pilots. It is increased M&T delivery capacity with a visible line from project problem to governed capability to realized value.

I have made that thinking concrete at https://russelldudek.github.io/Bechtel/. The candidate vision includes an interactive AI Capability Work Package, a detailed 90-day entry plan, an evidence map, and the questions I would use to learn Bechtel’s actual operating context before prescribing.

My background is adjacent to EPC rather than a claim of Bechtel-specific tenure. What transfers directly is the discipline: high-consequence engineering and manufacturing, quality systems, Lean/Gemba operating mechanisms, regulated field work, cross-functional technical translation, and hands-on AI workflow design. I would pair that experience with deliberate field learning and respect for Bechtel’s technical authorities, project processes, and customer obligations.

I would welcome the opportunity to discuss where M&T most needs help distinguishing a promising local use case from a repeatable enterprise-ready capability—and what evidence would make this role unquestionably valuable in its first year.
