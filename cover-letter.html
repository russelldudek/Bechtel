Bechtel’s AI Enablement Lead is not being hired to create more AI activity. The role exists to make distributed project demand governable, reusable, and measurable - without slowing the engineering and project judgment that protects safety, quality, schedule, and customer commitments.

That mandate matches the work I have repeatedly chosen. At Vape-Jet, I lead AI-first operating transformation across production, purchasing, quality, support, customer success, engineering issue flow, ERP/Odoo, Jira, HubSpot, Slack, telemetry, and machine-fleet feedback. Through DudeWorth, I develop AI opportunity and readiness frameworks that connect business value, feasibility, data, governance, human authority, adoption burden, reuse, and time-to-value.

At Compunetics, I led engineering, manufacturing, quality, customer delivery, and complex programs in advanced electronics and high-reliability environments. At Amazon, I built the operating routines, visibility, standard work, and escalation needed for scaled 24/7 delivery. Those experiences taught me that emerging technology only creates durable value when the workflow owner, evidence, authority boundary, support model, and improvement cadence are explicit.

I would bring Bechtel an operator’s approach to AI enablement: begin with a decision-critical workflow; identify the owner, consequence, evidence, and authority boundary; deliver the smallest controlled intervention; measure what changes in real work; and convert successful elements into reusable standards, training, and portfolio learning. The operating model is demonstrated at https://russelldudek.github.io/Bechtel.

My background is adjacent to EPC rather than a claim of direct Bechtel tenure. What transfers directly is the discipline: high-consequence engineering and manufacturing, quality systems, Lean/Gemba operating mechanisms, regulated field work, cross-functional technical translation, and hands-on AI workflow design. I would pair that experience with deliberate field learning and respect for Bechtel’s technical authorities, project processes, and customer obligations.

I would welcome the opportunity to discuss where M&T most needs help distinguishing a promising local use case from a repeatable enterprise-ready capability - and what evidence would make this role unquestionably valuable in its first year.
