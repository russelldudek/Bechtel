Why this role matters now

Bechtel has already declared the direction.

In February 2026, Bechtel formalized enterprise-wide EPC transformation leadership to accelerate AI, automation, robotics, and advanced digital delivery. M&T is simultaneously operating in high-growth, schedule-sensitive markets including semiconductors, data centers, batteries, and life sciences. The enablement lead is the translation and portfolio-execution layer between that enterprise ambition and project reality.

Enterprise sponsorship is visible.

Centralized EPC transformation leadership now connects mature digital tools, execution data, AI, robotics, and real-world deployment.

M&T workflows are unforgiving.

Advanced manufacturing and AI infrastructure projects reward speed and repeatability, but not at the expense of safety, quality, configuration control, or accountable engineering judgment.

Digital delivery already has proof points.

Bechtel publicly describes 100% digital delivery, equipment-to-map visibility, robotics, autonomous equipment, drones, and real-time field decisions. AI enablement must compound those operating strengths rather than become a parallel innovation theater.

First 90 days

Earn the right to scale.

01

Read the work

  • Map active AI demand, owners, vendors, data boundaries, and duplicate efforts.
  • Shadow representative workflows across engineering, project controls, construction, and handover.
  • Agree on intake fields, decision rights, and value-evidence categories.
Output: visible portfolio baseline + two bounded work-package candidates.
02

Prove the package

  • Run controlled builds with workflow owners and the Data Architecture & AI team.
  • Document human authority, failure modes, escalation, support, and ownership.
  • Instrument workflow evidence before adoption claims.
Output: evidence-backed disposition: advance, revise, hold, reuse, or stop.
03

Standardize the learning

  • Convert successful elements into reusable patterns, guidance, and training.
  • Establish portfolio review cadence and value-realization reporting.
  • Publish lessons learned without turning governance into a tollbooth.
Output: first reusable capability standard + 12-month portfolio logic.

The fit question

Can an operator-technologist translate into EPC quickly enough to create value?

Start with the workflow, not a claim of domain omniscience.

The first move is listening: project walk-throughs, artifact review, decision mapping, and explicit discovery with engineering, construction, controls, quality, and information-management leaders.

Bring a proven mechanism-building pattern.

Russell’s verified through-line is making complex work visible, connecting technical and business teams, creating standard work and decision systems, and turning emerging technology into adopted operating outcomes.

Respect technical authority.

The model makes engineering and project accountability more visible; it does not substitute AI confidence for professional judgment, customer requirements, configuration control, or safety and quality obligations.

Use adjacent high-consequence experience honestly.

Advanced electronics, manufacturing, robotics, regulated drone operations, quality systems, Lean operations, and scaled delivery provide a credible day-one base without inventing direct Bechtel tenure.

Questions worth asking before prescribing.

Where does M&T currently lose the most time distinguishing a local project need from a reusable GBU capability?
