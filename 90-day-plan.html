90-Day Entry Plan · AI Enablement Lead
Independent candidate material
AI Enablement Lead
Operating objective

Earn the right to scale.

Build a truthful portfolio view, prove two bounded work packages in real workflows, and leave Bechtel with reusable capability and a decision-closing cadence.

01
Days 1-30

Read the work

Establish a truthful picture of demand, workflow friction, ownership, architecture, governance, and existing capability.

Priority actions

  • Map active AI initiatives, pilots, vendors, owners, sponsors, and disposition.
  • Shadow representative workflows across engineering, controls, construction, information management, quality, and handover.
  • Align with Data Architecture & AI on platforms, security, evaluation, support, and escalation.
  • Define lightweight intake and work-package completeness standards.
  • Select two bounded candidates with named owners and measurable consequences.

Outputs

  • Current-state AI portfolio map
  • Stakeholder and decision-rights map
  • Work-package intake standard
  • Two discovery briefs with baseline evidence
GateStakeholders recognize the portfolio view as accurate.
02
Days 31-60

Prove the package

Deliver controlled real-work tests that produce evidence, learning, and ownership - not showcase demos.

Priority actions

  • Run discovery workshops at artifact and decision level.
  • Choose the smallest useful intervention: guidance, copilot, agent, low-code, automation, model, or no build.
  • Define authority, fallback, provenance, evaluation, support, and change-control expectations.
  • Instrument value and correction signals inside the workflow cadence.
  • Capture reusable components and lessons during the build.

Outputs

  • Two controlled workflow interventions
  • Evaluation and authority records
  • Value baseline and evidence plan
  • Initial reusable pattern library
GateReal-work use produces evidence for explicit disposition.
03
Days 61-90

Standardize learning

Turn evidence into a durable portfolio cadence, reusable capability, and practical adoption system.

Priority actions

  • Review results with project owners, Digital Solutions, Data Architecture & AI, and functions.
  • Disposition each initiative: advance, revise, reuse, hold, stop, or transfer.
  • Package successful components with ownership, training, support, evaluation, and value receipts.
  • Launch portfolio review and value-realization reporting.
  • Recommend 12-month sequencing grounded in demand clusters, reuse, consequence, readiness, and adoption burden.

Outputs

  • First reusable capability work package
  • Portfolio review and value cadence
  • Guidance and training starter set
  • 12-month sequencing recommendation

RF-BCH-90-02 · OPERATING CADENCE
Russell Dudek
90-Day Entry Plan · Governance, Measures, and Risk
Independent candidate material
AI Enablement Lead
Cadence design

Close decisions without turning governance into a tollbooth.

Operating posture

Listen before standardizing. Build with workflow owners. Scale proven patterns, not enthusiasm.

Working cadence

Weekly · work-package review

Owners, enablement, architecture, and affected functions review completeness, blockers, evidence, and next decision.

Biweekly · controlled build review

Inspect source fidelity, human authority, failure modes, user correction, support, and real-work observations.

Monthly · portfolio disposition

Advance, revise, reuse, hold, stop, or transfer. Publish decisions and retained lessons.

Quarterly · value and reuse review

Confirm realized outcomes, retained adoption, support burden, reusable standards, and sequencing changes.

Primary risks and controls
Innovation theater

Require workflow owner, baseline, real-work evidence, and explicit disposition.

Governance drag

Use lightweight completeness fields and time-boxed decisions, not committee accumulation.

Hidden authority transfer

Make approval, fallback, escalation, and stop conditions visible in the work package.

Local success that will not travel

Separate reusable core from project, customer, location, and contract variation.

Discovery and sequencing

Questions that change the plan

  • Which initiatives already contain reusable components?
  • Where is duplicate demand hidden by different functional language?
  • Which controls are non-negotiable, and where is local discretion expected?
  • What would cause an owner to stop using the capability?
  • Who owns the solution after enablement steps away?

12-month sequencing logic

  • Consequence and recurrence of the workflow problem
  • Readiness of owner, data, evidence, and support
  • Reuse potential across projects and functions
  • Adoption burden and change capacity
  • Architecture fit and resource requirement

What I will not do

  • Launch a general-purpose bot without a bounded workflow.
  • Treat low-code as low-governance.
  • Call technical success an outcome before workflow evidence.
  • Standardize a local variation before understanding why it exists.
